## 1. Brand Foundation

### 1.1 Brand Idea

Zelnoo is a diagnostics marketplace and operations platform that makes pathology and radiology discovery, comparison, booking, sample collection, report access, and partner coordination more transparent, reliable, and accessible.

Zelnoo should feel:

| Attribute | Meaning for Zelnoo |
|---|---|
| **Trusted** | Healthcare-grade, process-driven, transparent, reliable. |
| **Accessible** | Easy to understand for customers, doctors, labs, phlebotomists, and non-technical users. |
| **Premium** | Clean, modern, disciplined, minimal, and polished. |
| **Operational** | Built for sample collection, logistics, reports, partner management, and service accountability. |
| **Human** | Warm, reassuring, and service-focused, not cold or overly clinical. |

### 1.2 Brand Positioning Statement

**Zelnoo is a diagnostics platform that helps customers discover, compare, and book trusted pathology and radiology services with transparent pricing, doorstep collection, reliable logistics, and digital report access.**

### 1.3 Brand Promise

**Transparent diagnostics. Reliable collection. Trusted reports.**

### 1.4 Brand Personality

Zelnoo should communicate like a professional healthcare technology company, not like a discount marketplace. The tone should be warm but not casual, confident but not exaggerated, and simple but not generic.

| Do | Avoid |
|---|---|
| Clear healthcare language | Overly technical medical jargon for customers |
| Trust-building phrases | Fear-based medical messaging |
| Transparent pricing and process language | “Cheapest lab” positioning |
| Calm, confident, service-led tone | Flashy, noisy, discount-heavy tone |
| Compliance-aware wording | Medical diagnosis or treatment claims |

### 1.5 Core Messaging Pillars

| Pillar | Message Direction | Example Copy |
|---|---|---|
| **Transparency** | Pricing, TAT, lab information, and booking flow should be clear. | “Compare trusted diagnostic options before you book.” |
| **Convenience** | Doorstep sample collection and simple booking. | “Book tests from home in a few taps.” |
| **Trust** | Verified labs, trained phlebotomists, chain-of-custody, report access. | “Collected carefully. Delivered securely.” |
| **Choice** | Customers can compare labs, prices, TAT, and service options. | “Choose the diagnostic center that works for you.” |
| **Continuity** | Family profiles, report vault, repeat bookings, long-term health records. | “Keep your family’s reports organized in one place.” |

## 2. Brand Architecture

### 2.1 Master Brand

The master brand is **Zelnoo**. All products, portals, departments, templates, and offline materials must use Zelnoo as the primary brand name.

| Format | Usage |
|---|---|
| **Zelnoo** | Standard written form in sentences and documents. |
| **ZELNOO** | Logo/wordmark, navigation, badges, labels, caps, bags, and brand display. |
| **Zelnoo Private Limited** | Legal, HR, vendor, tax, bank, invoice, agreement, and compliance documents. |

### 2.2 Portal / Stakeholder Architecture

Zelnoo may use portal labels for clarity, but these are not separate brands. They are product areas under the Zelnoo master brand.

| Portal / Department | Approved Label | Primary Audience | Visual Treatment |
|---|---|---|---|
| Consumer App | **Zelnoo Consumer** | Customers and families | Primary orange, friendly cards, clear CTAs. |
| Phlebotomist App | **Zelnoo Partner App** or **Zelnoo Phlebo App** | Phlebotomists / collection partners | Orange + operational teal accents. |
| Lab Portal | **Zelnoo Lab Portal** | Diagnostic centers, labs, radiology centers | Orange + deep amber + trust badges. |
| Doctor Portal | **Zelnoo Doctor Portal** | Doctors and referring clinicians | Orange + trust blue accents. |
| Admin Console | **Zelnoo Admin Console** | Operations, quality, support, finance | Neutral dark UI with orange highlights. |
| Corporate / B2B | **Zelnoo Corporate** | Employers, insurers, corporate clients | Premium black, white, orange, minimal charts. |
| Internal HR / Finance | **Zelnoo Internal** | Employees, vendors, management | Clean white templates, black text, orange headers. |

### 2.3 Naming Rules

Approved names:

- Zelnoo App
- Zelnoo Lab Portal
- Zelnoo Doctor Portal
- Zelnoo Admin Console
- Zelnoo Partner App
- Zelnoo Reports Vault
- Zelnoo Corporate Health

Avoid:

- New sub-brand names without approval.
- Informal abbreviations in external material.
- Portal names that sound like separate companies.
- Mixed casing such as “ZelNoo”, “Zelno”, or “Zelnoo Healths”.

## 3. Logo System

### 3.1 Logo Description

The current Zelnoo logo system consists of:

1. A circular amber-orange icon with a stylized **Z** form.
2. A bold uppercase **ZELNOO** wordmark.
3. The tagline **Diagnostics, democratized.**
4. Optional diagnostic line-art symbols such as a test tube or droplet for marketing layouts.

The logo should always appear clean, premium, and uncluttered.

### 3.2 Approved Logo Variants

| Variant | Description | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| **Primary Horizontal Lockup** | Icon + ZELNOO wordmark | Website header, app splash, business card, letterhead, email signature, pitch deck cover. |
| **Stacked Lockup** | Icon above wordmark | Social display, posters, onboarding screens, merchandise, app launch screen. |
| **Icon Only** | Circular Z icon | App icon, favicon, ID card, uniform chest mark, bag mark, social profile image. |
| **Wordmark Only** | ZELNOO text | Narrow spaces, document headers, internal dashboards. |
| **Tagline Lockup** | Logo + “Diagnostics, democratized.” | Brand introductions, brochure cover, website hero, pitch decks. |
| **Monochrome Logo** | Black or white version | Legal docs, embossing, single-colour printing, laser engraving. |

### 3.3 Logo Clearspace

Use the height of the letter **Z** in the wordmark as the minimum clearspace on all sides.

For icon-only usage, use **25% of the icon diameter** as clearspace.

### 3.4 Minimum Logo Sizes

| Medium | Primary Lockup Minimum | Icon Minimum | Notes |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Mobile app | 120 px wide | 32 px | Splash screen may use larger lockup. |
| Web header | 140 px wide | 32 px | Keep crisp on retina displays. |
| Email signature | 130 px wide | 28 px | Avoid oversized logo in email. |
| Business card | 32 mm wide | 8 mm | Use vector artwork. |
| ID card | 28 mm wide | 8 mm | Ensure legibility after lamination. |
| Uniform embroidery | 70 mm wide | 20 mm | Use simplified vector logo. |
| Bag printing | 90 mm wide | 25 mm | Use high-contrast single-colour or full-colour logo. |

### 3.5 Logo Placement

| Asset | Preferred Placement |
|---|---|
| Website header | Top left. |
| App splash screen | Center. |
| Social post | Top left or bottom center depending on template. |
| Visiting card | Front center or top left; back center for premium card. |
| Polo T-shirt | Left chest; optional small back-neck mark. |
| Cap | Center front. |
| Phlebotomist bag | Front visible panel; avoid seams and zippers. |
| Email signature | Left or top of signature block. |
| Invoice / PO | Top left header. |
| Lab report wrapper / booking summary | Top left with document title on right. |

### 3.6 Correct Logo Usage

| Background | Logo Version |
|---|---|
| White / off-white | Full-colour logo or orange wordmark. |
| Black / dark background | Full-colour icon with white or orange wordmark. |
| Orange background | Black or white logo only; avoid orange wordmark on orange. |
| Photo background | Use logo inside a white/black container or apply overlay for contrast. |
| Printed black-and-white documents | Black monochrome logo. |

### 3.7 Incorrect Logo Usage

Do not:

- Stretch, compress, skew, rotate, or distort the logo.
- Change the wordmark letter spacing manually.
- Add shadows, glow, bevels, outlines, or 3D effects unless part of an approved master artwork.
- Place the orange logo on low-contrast orange/yellow backgrounds.
- Use the icon as a watermark behind dense text.
- Recreate the wordmark in another font.
- Mix the old amber `#F5B400` with the new primary `#F08E04` in the same asset.
- Use unofficial logo exports from WhatsApp screenshots or compressed images.
- Place the logo too close to document edges, stitching, folds, handles, or zippers.

### 3.8 Logo File Requirements

| Format | Usage |
|---|---|
| SVG | Website, app, Figma, print master, vector editing. |
| PDF | Vendor print handoff, professional printing. |
| PNG transparent | Email signature, social, MS Office, WhatsApp, quick use. |
| JPG | Only for non-transparent image previews. |
| ICO / PNG favicon | Browser favicon and app shortcuts. |
| EPS / AI / CDR | Print vendors, embroidery, screen printing, packaging. |

### 3.9 Logo Naming Convention

### 4.3 Brand Gradients

**Primary gradient:**

```css
background: linear-gradient(135deg, #FABA0A 0%, #F08E04 45%, #D97704 100%);
```

**Soft gradient:**

```css
background: linear-gradient(135deg, #FFF3D6 0%, #FFFFFF 45%, #FFE3A3 100%);
```

**Dark premium gradient:**

```css
background: radial-gradient(circle at top right, rgba(240,142,4,0.24), transparent 42%), #000000;
```

### 4.4 Colour Usage Ratio

| Colour Group | Usage Share | Notes |
|---|---:|---|
| White / off-white / neutral background | 55–65% | Keeps the product clean and healthcare-friendly. |
| Black / near-black | 15–20% | Adds premium contrast and strong hierarchy. |
| Primary orange | 10–15% | Use for CTA, highlights, status emphasis, brand anchor. |
| Supporting greys | 10–15% | Text hierarchy, borders, tables, metadata. |
| Semantic colours | 3–5% | Only for status states, not branding. |

## 5. Typography System

### 5.1 Primary Typefaces

| Role | Typeface | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| Display / Headings | **Syne** | Brand headlines, section titles, campaign messages, pitch decks. |
| Body / UI | **DM Sans** | App UI, website, documents, paragraphs, forms, tables. |
| Mono / Data | **DM Mono** | IDs, codes, report numbers, tokens, technical documentation. |

### 5.2 Fallback Fonts

| Context | Fallback |
|---|---|
| Web | `Syne`, `DM Sans`, `Inter`, `system-ui`, `sans-serif` |
| Mobile | `DM Sans`, `Inter`, `system-ui`, `sans-serif` |
| MS Office | Aptos / Arial when brand fonts are unavailable |
| Print Vendor | Use outlined text in vector files when necessary |

### 5.3 Type Hierarchy — Product UI

| Style | Font | Weight | Size | Line Height | Usage |
|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Display | Syne | 700/800 | 48–64 px | 1.08 | Website hero, campaign headline. |
| H1 | Syne | 700 | 36–44 px | 1.15 | Page title, dashboard title. |
| H2 | Syne | 650/700 | 28–32 px | 1.2 | Section title. |
| H3 | Syne | 600 | 22–24 px | 1.3 | Card group title. |
| H4 | DM Sans | 600 | 18–20 px | 1.4 | Card title, form section title. |
| Body Large | DM Sans | 400/500 | 18 px | 1.6 | Hero paragraph, page intro. |
| Body | DM Sans | 400 | 16 px | 1.6 | Main content and forms. |
| Body Small | DM Sans | 400 | 14 px | 1.5 | Metadata, helper text, cards. |
| Caption | DM Sans | 500 | 12 px | 1.4 | Labels, badges, timestamps. |
| Micro | DM Sans | 600 | 11 px | 1.3 | Uppercase labels and status chips. |
| Code/Data | DM Mono | 400/500 | 13–14 px | 1.5 | Booking ID, sample ID, API token, report ID. |

### 5.4 Typography Rules

Do:

- Use Syne for important brand headings only.
- Use DM Sans for most product and document text.
- Use DM Mono only for technical, ID, or data-style text.
- Keep body text at 16 px minimum in digital products.
- Use clear spacing between heading and body copy.

Avoid:

- Using too many font weights in one screen.
- Using decorative fonts in healthcare communication.
- Using all caps for long sentences.
- Using thin font weights on orange backgrounds.
- Using low-contrast grey text for important information.

### 5.5 Wordmark Rule

Do not recreate the **ZELNOO** wordmark using typed text in Syne or any other font. Always use the approved vector wordmark file.

## 6. Layout, Spacing, and Visual Style

### 6.1 Design Personality

Zelnoo layouts should feel clean, premium, and operationally credible.

Core visual characteristics:

- Large whitespace.
- Clear hierarchy.
- Rounded but not childish corners.
- Sharp alignment.
- Minimal gradients.
- Premium black/orange contrast.
- Healthcare-safe imagery.
- Calm, high-trust composition.

### 6.2 Spacing Scale

Use an 8-point spacing system.

| Token | Value | Usage |
|---|---:|---|
| `space-1` | 4 px | Micro gaps. |
| `space-2` | 8 px | Label-to-field spacing. |
| `space-3` | 12 px | Small card internals. |
| `space-4` | 16 px | Standard component padding. |
| `space-5` | 20 px | Card body spacing. |
| `space-6` | 24 px | Section/card spacing. |
| `space-8` | 32 px | Major content blocks. |
| `space-10` | 40 px | Large sections. |
| `space-12` | 48 px | Page-level separation. |
| `space-16` | 64 px | Hero and landing sections. |

### 6.3 Border Radius

| Token | Value | Usage |
|---|---:|---|
| `radius-sm` | 6 px | Chips, small labels. |
| `radius-md` | 10 px | Inputs, dropdowns. |
| `radius-lg` | 12 px | Buttons. |
| `radius-xl` | 16 px | Cards. |
| `radius-2xl` | 20 px | Large cards / modals. |
| `radius-full` | 999 px | Pills, avatars, badges. |

### 6.4 Shadows

Use shadows sparingly. Healthcare interfaces should feel clean and not overly decorative.

| Token | Shadow | Usage |
|---|---|---|
| `shadow-sm` | `0 1px 2px rgba(0,0,0,0.06)` | Inputs, light surfaces. |
| `shadow-md` | `0 4px 12px rgba(0,0,0,0.08)` | Cards, dropdowns. |
| `shadow-lg` | `0 12px 32px rgba(0,0,0,0.12)` | Modals, floating panels. |
| `shadow-orange` | `0 8px 24px rgba(240,142,4,0.22)` | Primary CTA, selected premium cards only. |

## 7. Accessibility Basics

| Area | Rule |
|---|---|
| Text contrast | Minimum 4.5:1 for body text. |
| Touch targets | Minimum 44 × 44 px. |
| Body text | 16 px minimum for consumer app/web. |
| Form errors | Use red + text explanation, not colour alone. |
| Status | Use icon/label + colour. |
| QR codes | Must be tested after printing and resizing. |
| Motion | Avoid excessive animation; respect reduced-motion settings. |
